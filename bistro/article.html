Low and Slow

This week I am extremely excited about a new cooking technique called sous vide. [cite: 7]

In sous vide cooking, you submerge the food (usually vacuum-sealed in plastic) into a water bath that is precisely set to the target temperature you want the food to be cooked to. [cite: 8]

In his book, Cooking for Geeks, Jeff Potter describes it as ultra-low-temperature poaching. [cite: 9]
